Prince Louis Rwagasore

former Prime Minister of Burundi

Introduction

Prince Louis Rwagasore was a Burundian politician who served as the second prime minister of Burundi for a brief period from September 28, 1961, until his assassination. He was a prominent figure in the country's independence movement and was murdered just over 60 years ago, which involved the complicity of Belgium during a tumultuous period in Burundi's history.

Timeline

  • Life and Education

    January 10, 1932: Prince Louis Rwagasore is born in Gitega, Burundi, as the son of King Mwambutsa IV. 1945-1951: Rwagasore attends secondary school in modern-day Rwanda and later studies in Belgium at the Université catholique de Louvain.

  • Involvement and Leadership

    1956: Rwagasore becomes actively involved in Burundi's nationalist movement, advocating for independence from Belgian colonial rule. 1957: He founds the Union for National Progress (Union pour le Progrès National, UPRONA), a political party aimed at achieving independence for Burundi.

  • Path to Independence

    1960: UPRONA wins a majority in legislative elections, but Belgian authorities annul the results due to fears of instability. 1961: New elections are held on September 18, 1961. UPRONA, under Rwagasore’s leadership, wins a significant majority, gaining control of the Legislative Assembly.

  • Assassination and Aftermath

    October 13, 1961: Prince Louis Rwagasore is assassinated in Bujumbura, Burundi, by a Greek national named Jean Kageorgis, who was hired by political rivals. His death sparks outrage and mourning across the nation. July 1, 1962: Burundi gains independence from Belgium. Rwagasore is posthumously honored as a national hero and a key figure in the independence movement.

  • Legacy

    Post-Independence: Rwagasore’s assassination is seen as a pivotal moment in Burundi’s history, and his vision of a united and independent Burundi continues to influence the country’s political landscape. Annual Commemoration: October 13 is observed as a national holiday in Burundi, known as Prince Louis Rwagasore Day, in memory of his contributions and sacrifice for the nation's independence. This timeline provides an overview of the significant events in the life and legacy of Prince Louis Rwagasore, a key figure in Burundi's struggle for independence.

Genealogy

Prince Louis Rwagasore, born on January 10, 1932, in Gitega, Burundi, was the son of King Mwambutsa IV Bangiricenge of Burundi and Queen Thérèse Kanyonga. King Mwambutsa IV, a significant figure in Burundian history, reigned from 1915 to 1966 and was instrumental in navigating the country through the colonial era towards independence. Louis Rwagasore was part of the royal Ganwa dynasty, which has a long lineage of rulers and significant political influence in the region. His paternal lineage connects him to a succession of Burundian kings who played crucial roles in the historical and cultural shaping of the nation. King Mwambutsa IV himself was a descendant of King Mwezi IV Gisabo, who resisted German colonial forces in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Rwagasore married Marie-Rose Ntamikevyo in 1959, and they had two daughters, Princesses Marie-Gisèle and Marie-Eshe. Despite his untimely assassination in 1961, Rwagasore’s legacy continues through his descendants and the ongoing influence of the royal family in Burundi's cultural and historical context. His vision for an independent and united Burundi has cemented his status as a national hero, with his descendants upholding his legacy in various capacities within the nation.
